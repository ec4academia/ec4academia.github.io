Incentives in Academia

1st Annual Workshop on Incentives in Academia

An EC-24 Workshop, July 8th, New Haven

Overview

Strategic and incentive problems are ubiquitous in academia. This not only provides a set of applications such as peer review and peer grading but also provides a testing ground for more general Econ-CS theories. This workshop seeks to bring together both researchers working on academic applications where accounting for strategic reasoning is important (both Econ-CS research and those from other fields such as HCI, EdTech, education, OR, TCS, etc) and Econ-CS researchers whose theories might be tested in academic applications.

Call For Papers

The organizing committee is looking forward to your contributions to the 1st workshop on Incentives in Academia, held in conjunction with EC’24 on July 8th in New Haven. We encourage submissions of research papers whose methods, models, or key insights are 1) relevant to a scenario in academia and 2) can speak with strategic behaviors or incentives.

For example, relevant empirical research includes designing experiments and surveys to assess various teaching methodologies or student feedback systems. Additionally, papers that utilize data analysis to understand incentives in academic contexts — such as peer review, grading, and learner-sourcing — are encouraged. On the theoretical front, insights from information elicitation could enhance the honesty and quality of grading reports, auction theory might optimize peer review processes, and matching market design could improve student admissions, paper bidding, and faculty job market strategies.

Topics of interest include but are not limited to:

  • Incentives in peer review
  • Strategic Behaviors in Classrooms
  • Learner-sourcing
  • Mechanisms for teaching evaluations
  • Mechanisms for grading
  • Job market design
We look forward to your contributions and an engaging exchange of ideas at the workshop!

Submission Guidelines

We accept a wide range of submission formats, such as extended abstracts 9-page AI papers, longer conference papers (e.g. papers rejected from EC-24), or journal papers. The workshop is non-archival. Recent papers that have been accepted, under review, and in preparation are all welcome to submit. Although we are flexible in both the format and the length of the submissions, we insist that the main contribution should be identifiable in the first 6 pages of the paper (3 pages for double-column papers).

Papers should be submitted through EasyChair.

Submissions will be lightly reviewed for poster presentations. Depending on the volume, a few selected papers will be orally presented. The primary selection criteria will be the relevance to the workshop's topic. We, therefore, encourage authors to highlight the connections either in their papers or in the submission form on EasyChair.
